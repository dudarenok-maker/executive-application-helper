## Section A — Core positioning blocks

Section A is grouped into three clusters: **Identity and positioning** (typically A1, A2, A6, A7, A8, A9), **Motivation / why this role** (typically A3, A4, A5, and A12 for career-pivot framings) and **Leadership approach** (typically A10 and A11).

> **Setup guidance:** Section A holds the most reusable blocks in the bank. Every application starts here. Aim for three to five strong positioning blocks during initial setup; deepen the rest application by application. Use the entry format defined in `Examples_Master.md` Section 2. Below is a single worked skeleton showing the structure — duplicate it for each block and customise.

### A-II. Motivation / why this role

> **Setup guidance:** Capture two to four motivation framings, each tuned to a different sector or role-family bucket the candidate targets. The motivation framings carry a lot of weight because they answer the panel's core question: why this role, why now, why you. Match each framing to its trigger.
