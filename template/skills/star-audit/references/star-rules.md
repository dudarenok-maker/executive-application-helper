# STAR construction rules — canonical reference (rev 2026-06-12, codifies the framework / V4.5 conventions)

## The framework reframe — Why = S+T, How = A, What = R

Standard STAR treats Action as "what I did". For executive interviews that is not enough:

- **Why** = Situation + Task — why this work was needed, why I was there.
- **How** = Action — **the mechanism, the sequencing, the judgement call**, not just "what I did".
- **What** = Result — what changed, with metrics.

The Action's job is to demonstrate executive judgement. "Took the work to market" is *what*; "took the work to market via an open EOI to test the field, then a structured RFQ that **asked vendors to design the operational model, not just price the services** — that change alone changed who responded" is *how*. Sharpen Actions on HOW: mechanism, sequencing, judgement, principle.

**Audit test:** an Action that can be reduced to a comma-separated list of activities with no ordering rationale or judgement call is a what-list. Flag it.

## The 5.8 STAR style — flowing prose with embedded principle lines

Structure:

> [Brief opening sentence framing the HOW]. [First mechanism], followed by [principle / why it worked]. [Second mechanism], followed by [principle / why it worked]. [Third mechanism], followed by [principle / why it worked]. **[Closing crisp principle.]**

- **Not numbered lists.** A numbered or bulleted Action is a High-severity finding.
- Embedded principle lines are **bolded** — they are the load-bearing structure the candidate holds in memory under pressure.
- The response ends on a crisp closing principle, bolded.
- Named after the STAR in the originating framework's panel brief that was first written in this form.

## On-the-fly drop list — per spoken response

Every spoken STAR in a speaking-notes addendum must carry a drop list:

| Response | First sentence to drop if pace is tight | Second cut option |
|---|---|---|
| Q1 | [a specific sentence — colour, parenthetical or repetition] (saves ~10–15 sec) | [another specific sentence] (saves ~8–12 sec) |

- Each response needs **at least 20 seconds of cuttable runway** (sum of stated savings).
- Cuts must name specific sentences, not categories.
- **Never cut the closing principle lines** — a drop-list entry naming a bolded principle or the closing principle is a High-severity finding.

## Severity scale

| Severity | Meaning | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **High** | Structural breach — blocks delivery in gate mode | Numbered-list Action; missing closing principle; principle line in the drop list |
| **Medium** | Quality erosion — advisory | Unbolded principles; what-list Action with partial mechanism; drop-list runway < 20 sec |
| **Low** | Awareness | Result metrics present but stale vs `Examples_Section_6_Signature_Metrics.md` phrasing |
