## Section C — Ready-made templates

This section contains reusable drafting templates and a short-form bank. Each template includes a worked exemplar paragraph illustrating voice, cadence and evidence density, so Claude can both follow the structure and mirror the style.

> **Setup guidance:** The C templates below are largely portable across candidates — the structural prose is the operating logic and rarely needs to change. Two adjustments are typical at setup:
>
> 1. **Rename templates** that reference jurisdictions or sectors the candidate doesn't operate in (e.g. C3 references "NSW" by default — rename to whatever jurisdiction or framing the candidate uses).
> 2. **Replace the worked-exemplar paragraphs.** Each template includes a `[Worked exemplar paragraph (for voice calibration)]` placeholder that needs the candidate's own worked exemplar dropped in once at least four B-section entries exist. Until then, leave the placeholder marked as "to be populated" and Claude will fall back to extracting an exemplar from the available B-section entries at draft time.
>
> The unique value of Section C is the **structural prose** — the worked-exemplar paragraphs are calibration aids, not the primary content.

**Editorial note:** Unlike Sections A and B, C entries combine **instructional guidance** (how to structure the response) and **worked-exemplar model text** (usable draft material). Tightening a model paragraph reduces its value as drop-in material — preserve worked exemplars verbatim once they exist.

#### C2. [Behavioural response template — typical: 300-word capped behavioural response for high-scrutiny roles]

- **Tier:** Primary (template)
- **Last updated:** [YYYY-MM-DD]
- **Tags:**
  - Role families: [Role families]
  - Capability: [Capabilities]
  - Sector: [Sectors]
  - Anchor org: Role-dependent — choose the strongest anchor per question
- **Primary example:** Reusable drafting format
- **Secondary example:** N/A
- **See also:** [Related B-section IDs]
- **Best used for:** Applications where responses are capped at 300 words; regulators, integrity agencies, oversight bodies and other high-scrutiny environments

**Core response — template**

Use a compact four-part structure:

1. Open with a direct thesis sentence that answers the question in principle, not the background.
2. Identify the two or three capabilities or priorities that matter most, using the role's language selectively.
3. Anchor the response in one or two concise examples with scope, action and outcome — focusing on governance, judgement, sequencing, stakeholder alignment, delivery discipline and public value.
4. Close with one sentence linking the example back to the target role's operating context, such as trust, defensibility, compliance, service continuity or regulatory impact.

This structure keeps the response strategic and readable under a tight cap, and reduces the risk of over-spending on scene-setting or chronology.

- Keep the opening sentence direct; avoid spending space on background.
- For regulator roles, use compliance-facing or legally sensitive examples where they are the strongest honest fit, rather than overstating direct enforcement experience.

**Core response — template**

Use a true cover-letter structure, but write it with the substance and discipline of a short statement of claims. Start with motivation and fit in one paragraph. #### C4. Targeted pitch — long form

- **Tier:** Primary (template)
- **Last updated:** [YYYY-MM-DD]
- **Tags:**
  - Role families: All
  - Capability: Cross-capability
  - Sector: Cross-sector
  - Anchor org: Role-dependent
- **Best used for:** Targeted pitches, executive summaries, recruiter-requested longer responses (600–1,000 words)

**Core response — template**

A long-form targeted pitch is a hybrid of cover letter and statement of claims, used when the recruiter or organisation asks for a "pitch", "expression of interest" or "summary of fit" rather than a formal cover letter or SoC. Structure: open with a single direct paragraph naming the candidate, the target role and the specific reason for fit (one or two sentences). Follow with three to five body paragraphs, each anchored in a single capability cluster — strategy, delivery, people, commercial, risk — using one strong example per paragraph. Close with a forward-momentum paragraph that names what the candidate brings to the first six to twelve months.

#### C5. Targeted pitch — short form

- **Tier:** Primary (template)
- **Last updated:** [YYYY-MM-DD]
- **Best used for:** Recruiter-facing pitches, executive hooks, LinkedIn-message-style introductions (100–200 words)

**Core response — template**

Three short paragraphs, total 100–200 words. Paragraph 1: one-sentence positioning + one-sentence reason for fit. Paragraph 2: one or two evidence anchors with quantified outcome. Paragraph 3: forward-facing close — what the candidate brings, what they want next.

#### C6. Recruiter screening response

- **Tier:** Primary (template)
- **Last updated:** [YYYY-MM-DD]
- **Best used for:** Inbound recruiter screens, LinkedIn replies (~50 words)

**Core response — template**

Two sentences, ideally. Sentence 1: confirm interest + one sentence of positioning anchored to the role. Sentence 2: ask for the next step — call, more information, candidate information pack. Match the recruiter's register; do not over-reach for formality.
